Sectional Doors

Sectional garage doors come in a wide range of sizes, from 8 foot wide to 24 foot wide and can be ever higher than 20 feet. The common style is a ribbed design with 24" high panels. Commercial garage doors are heavier than residential doors and interchangeable since there is only one design.

Rolling Steel

A rolling steel door is a durable, space-saving door made from interlocking slats of steel that roll up into a coil above the opening when opened. These doors are commonly used in commercial and industrial settings such as warehouses, factories, and storefronts. They are designed for heavy-duty use, offering excellent security, durability, and efficiency.

  • We determine the specific requirements, such as door size, material, and insulation needs, based on the facility's usage
  • We use high spec fastening systems to make sure reliable installation
  • We use trusted brands like Cookson and CHI

Roll-Ups

Commercial roll up doors are often called sheet doors. They have a seamless curtain that rolls around a drum. They are a cost effective solution to provide a secure and efficient entrance to you industrial or commercial setting.

  • The upside is they are relative inexpensive and the installation process is relatively inexpensive also
  • The downside is there isn't a whole lot you can do if the curtain gets ripped or bent. Rolling steel is more expensive but its slats can be replaced
  • Ideal for smaller scale needs, but not designed for high frequency use. Rolling steel or sectional doors are better for those purposes.
  • Rollup garage doors have a compact opening and closing mechanism that does not require extsra space.
